üìÖ Getting Started as a Beginner

To start calisthenics, focus on mastering foundational movements such as push-ups, squats, pull-ups, dips, and planks. These movements prepare your body for more advanced skills. Begin with easier variations if necessary, such as knee push-ups or negative pull-ups. Practice good form, start slow, and stay consistent. Progression in calisthenics takes time but becomes more rewarding as you unlock new abilities.

üî• Warm-Up (5‚Äì10 Minutes)

Before jumping into your main workout, always warm up your body. This improves circulation, loosens joints, and reduces injury risk. Here's a simple full-body warm-up:

  • 30 seconds Jumping Jacks
  • 20 seconds Arm Circles (forward & backward)
  • 20 seconds Shoulder Shrugs
  • 30 seconds High Knees
  • 30 seconds Bodyweight Squats
  • 10 Arm Swings (cross & open)
  • 20 seconds Hip Circles
  • 20 seconds Leg Swings (front & side)
  • 30 seconds Plank Hold
  • 15 seconds Neck Rolls (left & right)

Take short breaks between movements if needed. Once you're warmed up, your body will be primed for performance.

Planche

To unlock planche, master:
  • 60 sec Plank hold
  • 30 Clean Diamond Push-ups
  • 10 Planche leans (5 sec hold)

Front Lever

To unlock front lever, master:
  • 15 Pull-ups (full form)
  • 10 Skin-the-cat rotations
  • 3x 15s tuck front lever holds

Elbow Lever

To unlock elbow lever, master:
  • 30 Push-ups
  • 30s Crow pose hold
  • Strong core & arm balance
